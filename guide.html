SlimeVille is a browser-based multiplayer game where players control a slime character in an open world. Players gather resources, train skills, craft equipment, cook food, and fight increasingly powerful monsters and bosses.

New players spawn in the Village, which contains all essential services: a Bank, General Store, Blacksmith, Crafting Table, Campfire, and Food Shop.

Getting Started

  1. Click trees, rocks, or fishing spots to gather resources and earn XP.
  2. Sell gathered items at the General Store for coins.
  3. Buy your first Stone Tools at the Blacksmith (100 coins) for faster gathering.
  4. Cook raw fish at a Campfire for healing food.
  5. Craft combat gear at the Crafting Table and start fighting animals.

Skills

There are 9 trainable skills. Each has its own XP track and level. Total Level is the sum of all skill levels. Gathering skills have a fail chance that decreases with level (70% at level 1, approaching 0% at high levels).

Level Progression

Every skill uses the same XP formula: Level = floor(sqrt(XP / 34)) + 1. Leveling up improves your character in several ways:

BenefitHow it scales
Gathering success rateStarts at 30% (70% fail at level 1). Fail chance drops by 0.3% per level, reaching ~2% fail at high levels.
Hitpoints (max HP)+10 HP per Hitpoints level. Level 1 = 10 HP, level 100 = 1,000 HP, level 200 = 2,000 HP.
Combat damageHigher Melee/Ranged/Magic level + better weapon tier = more damage per hit.
Cooking burn chanceBurn chance decreases by 3% per Cooking level. A level 10 cook has ~0% burn on Shrimp.
Resource unlocksHigher-tier trees, rocks, and fish require a minimum skill level to gather.
Equipment unlocksBetter equipment tiers require a minimum combat level to equip.
XP per level increases quadratically. Level 10 needs 2,754 XP, level 50 needs 81,634 XP, level 100 needs 332,934 XP, level 200 needs 1,345,534 XP.

Party System

Click the handshake emote near another player to invite them to your party. Party members can enter boss instances together and share loot.

Food & Cooking

Click food in your inventory to eat it and restore HP. Raw fish and meat can be cooked at a Campfire (costs 3 logs per cook). Higher Cooking levels reduce burn chance. Burnt food heals nothing.

Quick Food Slot: Drag any food or potion onto the E slot below your inventory to set it as your quick food. Press E to instantly eat/use it without opening your inventory. Right-click the slot to clear it.

Multiplayer

SlimeVille is multiplayer by default. Other players appear as colored slimes in the game world.

  • See other players moving and gathering in real-time.
  • Use emotes to communicate (Emotes tab or F5).
  • Click another player to inspect their stats and equipment.
  • Form a party using the handshake emote to fight bosses together.
  • Trade items with other players via the trade system.

Tips

  • Upgrade tools as soon as you can afford them — the speed increase compounds over time.
  • Bank your items frequently. Your bag only holds 32 slots.
  • Always cook raw food before eating — cooked food heals significantly more.
  • Check the Task Board early for easy XP and coin rewards.
  • Prioritize ATK upgrades early in the game (weapons > armor).
  • Life Steal from the Washing Machine is a strong sustain option for bossing.
  • Horses can be mounted for 2x movement speed. Click a horse to ride, click again or press M to dismount.

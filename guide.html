SlimeVilleWiki

Play

You're a slime! You spawn in the village center. Click trees, rocks, or fishing spots to start gathering resources. Sell items at the General Store for coins. Level up skills, craft equipment, and take on bosses.

💤 Before you walk away or close the tab — click a fishing spot, rock, or tree first! Your slime keeps gathering on its own while you're AFK, so you'll come back to a stack of progress instead of nothing.
First goal: buy Stone Tools at the Blacksmith for 100 coins!

Combat Formulas

How damage and speed actually work

Damage you deal: random(1 .. 15 + floor(ATK × 0.5) + floor(skillLevel × 0.6)). ATK comes from your equipped weapon + accessories + stars. Skill level (Melee/Bow/Mage) is a major DPS lever — every level is +0.6 max hit.

Damage you take: max(1, incoming − DEF). DEF is flat damage resistance now — each armor piece subtracts its DEF value directly from incoming hits (Tin 1, Copper 2, ..., Celestial 12, up to Astral 30). No hidden percentage scaling. Stars on armor add a bit more DR. Some boss attacks have armor pen and only count 30–50% of your DR.

Attack speed: baked into the weapon tier — +10% per tier for the matching style. Tin sword swings at base speed (0.70s); Celestial sword at 2.10× speed (0.33s); Astral sword at 2.50× (0.28s). A Cactus bow accelerates bow attacks; a Celestial staff accelerates magic. Tempo (in-combat stack) and the Swiftness Potion still apply on top. Old Attack Speed potential lines have been retired.

TL;DR: levels and gear tier are now the primary DPS scalers. Stars and potentials are flavor on top, not the difference between “0 dmg” and “turbo god.”

Attack speed is now baked into your equipped weapon's tier. A Cactus bow swings bow attacks faster, a Celestial staff swings staff attacks faster — they don’t cross-pollinate. Bare-fist baseline = 1.00× (no weapon).

Coordinate clan goals

Global tasks

3 tasks shared by every clan

Three global tasks are active for every clan in the game — not a race. Each clan can complete each task independently by depositing matching items into its vault; when your clan hits the target, the reward is auto-claimed and added to your clan XP. Tasks rotate weekly.

Each task = item + qty (e.g. 600 Emberheart Wood)
Per-member cap: one player can contribute at most 50% of a task — spread the work
Reward: clan XP only — lifts the whole clan’s tier (does not add to individual member points)
Admins can reroll a slot any time

Frenzies

Random bonus events at resource nodes

While gathering, a frenzy can randomly trigger on your node. The node glows with magma cracks and grants 10x XP on your next successful gather, plus bonus drops.

Fish Frenzy: Fishing spots glow purple — 10x XP + Trophy Cowfish drop.

Mining Frenzy: Rock glows with lava cracks — 10x XP + 1–5 Gold Nuggets (or Meteor Cores on a meteor).

WC Frenzy: Tree glows — 10x XP + bonus drops.

🍀 Luck boosts frenzy chance: The Luck stat now increases frenzy rate — each 1% Luck = +1% frenzy chance. Stack Luck on washed gear to trigger frenzies more often.

💤

AFK Gathering — How Long?

Auto-gather while you alt-tab or window is unfocused

Click a resource to start gathering, then walk away — your slime keeps mining/chopping/fishing on its own. After your AFK timer expires, gathering stops automatically (you stay logged in, just no more progress on that node).

Visual cues:

  • The gather animation continues while AFK (chopping / mining swing)
  • XP & item floaters keep popping
  • The node still depletes (meat trees, meteors)
  • If the node depletes or the AFK timer fires, you stop — no warning, just silence
  • Tab back in — the catch-up runs instantly so your stats are up-to-date

Per-resource AFK windows:

Standard mining / WC / fishing12 minutes
All berry foraging6 hours
Elder / Golden / Crystal / Shadow / Ember / Frost / Void berries6 hours
Bamboo6 hours
Meat Tree6 hours
Meteor Mining6 hours
Flax / Hemp12 minutes

Note: any input (click, key, mouse move) resets your AFK timer to zero. Long AFK kicks in when you genuinely walk away. Items still cap at the node's HP — meteor and meat tree won't give more than they have.

☄️

Crashed Meteor

Roaming legendary mining node

A Crashed Meteor spawns at a random location on the map. All players share the same meteor — it’s server-synced. Mine it for Meteor Cores (legendary). The meteor has 600 HP and visually degrades through 6 tiers as it’s mined.

When depleted, a new meteor crashes at a different location after ~15 seconds. Check the minimap for its location (orange ☄️ icon).

XP scales with your mining level: 80 + 7.5 × level. Mining frenzies can trigger on the meteor — 10x XP and 1–5 bonus Meteor Cores!

🥩

Meat Tree

Depleting woodcutting node

Meat Trees are rare, server-synced trees that drop Prime Cuts (legendary). They deplete as players chop them, then respawn elsewhere.

XP scales with woodcutting level: 80 + 2.5 × level. Woodcutting frenzies can trigger here too!

🔮

Crop Circles (Faith)

Channel for orbs and bonus XP

Sacred crop circles appear around the map. Stand inside one and channel to charge up. The longer you channel (up to 2 minutes), the higher quality orb you receive.

Orbs grant a burst of Farming XP scaled by your channel time. Higher-quality orbs give more XP. Crop circles rotate locations periodically.

Find Pet Scrolls as rare drops while gathering. Use them in the Pet Garden to unlock perks. Each perk charges up over time.

How pets work:

  • Pets are bought from the Task Board → Pet Garden with Slime Coin (cheap, currently the only currency).
  • You can own up to 12 pets but only 1 can be equipped at a time. Unequipped pets stay in the garden and slowly gather stuff for you.

  • Release a pet to sell it back — no coin loss currently.
  • Resource scrolls (Woodcut/Mining/Fishing) feed your pet’s skilling levels. Pets also gain a little XP passively when you gather.
  • Pet traits (Lumberjack, Angler, Miner, Flat, Hardy…) are mostly cosmetic right now — stats were stripped. Lumberjack-style traits may grant 2x scroll bonuses in a future update.

Take equipment to the Blacksmith and enhance it with stars. Each star increases stats but has a chance to fail. Failed attempts can downgrade your item.

Enhancement cost scales with star level. Weapons benefit most from early stars — prioritize ATK over DEF early game.

☠️

Death & Loss

Don’t panic — nothing’s gone

If you die, you lose nothing — no items, no coins, no XP. You respawn in town and pick right back up. Push into dangerous zones without worry.

This goes for venturing far from town as a low-level too — the danger is just running out of food. The world is safe to explore at any combat level.

🌱

Compost & Charges

Feed the composter for farming charges

Toss any organic item (berries, crops, mushrooms, raw food) into the composter to earn compost charges. Better-tier inputs yield more charges per item.

Crops convert 1:1 — one crop = one charge, no bonus multiplier. The cheapest path to a lifetime supply is dumping a stack of basic berries.

⚖️

Refining Weapons

Upgrade the weapon you already have

Refining at the crafting table increases a weapon’s stats by dumping materials into the piece you already own. It’s an alternative to praying for a higher rarity roll on a re-craft — pick whichever path you’ve got the resources for.

Crafting itself can also randomly produce a higher-tier rarity, so spam-crafting end-game gear and refining the keeper is a strong combo.

🎒

XP Gear Set & Loadouts

Why veterans keep a spare set

XP potential is the rarest washer line in the game — only a flat 1% boost, no RNG range, and capped at 3 pieces per gear slot. Most players keep a separate XP-stacked set in a bank tab for skilling and swap to combat gear for fights.

Use bank tabs to organize loadouts so swapping the whole set takes a couple of clicks.

Bank tab presets: Each bank tab can be loaded as a preset for instant swapping. The B tab is special — it tries to equip your highest-DPS gear.

Mixing combat styles: Potential lines on a staff/bow/etc. always apply to you, regardless of the style you’re currently using. The weapon’s raw damage stats, however, only matter when you’re actually using that style.

👑

Amulets

Better neck slots exist

The Bone Amulet is the easy starter neck slot, but it’s not the only one. The Dragon boss drops the current best-in-slot amulet at a semi-rare rate. Check the Crafting Table for other amulets you can craft.

🐲

First Boss — What You Need

Lower the entry bar

The easiest boss is achievable with basic gear and a bit of combat training. AFK some Wolves or Ghosts first to top up your combat levels, bring a stack of food, and you should be fine. Don’t over-prepare — jump in.

🛠️

Tools by Skill

What needs a tool, what doesn’t

Only three skills require a tool: Mining (pickaxe), Woodcutting (axe), and Fishing (rod). Every other gathering skill (foraging berries, farming, etc.) works with bare hands.

Tool tiers don’t stop at Celestial — 4 new tiers were just added above it. Upgrade as soon as you can afford it; each tier is a big jump in gather speed and XP/hr.

🌍

World-Shared Resources

Meat Trees aren’t events

Meat Trees are not world events — they’re permanent world-shared nodes. There are 4 on the map at any time. When one’s depleted, it’s gone for everyone until it regrows.

The Crashed Meteor is also world-shared — check the minimap for both. Get there fast or someone else will finish it off.
